So let's talk about League of Legend's Flying Cat! (Yuumi)
[ A Character Destroyed by "Toxicity" ]

I should probably start by saying I actually like her quite a lot, and NOT JUST BECAUSE SHE'S A CAT! But rather, because her mechanics are fairly interesting, as a huge fan of enchanter-style supports (Sona/Nami/Lulu) I was bound to like this one given Riot's history, but she's actually one of my favorites. The reason for this is fairly simple, she's all about positioning and observing the situation rather than being some sort of brute force. This does however lead to her biggest downfall...

Yuumi can't do anything alone, and the community has branded her as "useless."

Now, to be fair she can still make plays similar to a good Sona or Janna when alone, but it's so much more exaggerated those any other champ in the game. However, let me explain Yuumi for those that don't actually know yet (or simply don't play League of Legends). All characters have some sort of role in League, each with their own skill sets and unique mechanics. Yuumi's special mechanic is that instead of moving on the field by herself she "attaches" to other characters and helps by amplifying their damage, giving heals/shields/speed buffs, and she even has some offensive skills she can cast herself while still attached and invulnerable! Sounds cool right? Lots of stuff in there, and given support items she has a huge toolkit, but all those tools are about helping others, and what makes "Yuumi can't do anything alone" such a prevalent sentence is that she has next to nothing "for herself."

And this is where League of Legends notoriously toxic community comes into play. Yuumi requires 100% cooperation from team mates to work in every aspect of her kit. Need to get somewhere? Attach to someone who's headed there for speed boosts, need to get out of a fight? Attach to someone on the outer ring. Need to slow someone that's chasing you? Attach to someone so you can empower your attack skill and slow them! But if no one's there you're.. well, dead. This also works for helping people as well, if they don't work with you and position properly you can't jump between people and do your job, making the champ look useless because people refuse to help you help them! This lead to Yuumi having under a 30% win-rate near launch and created a toxic nature where people started to troll anyone that picked Yuumi, only extending the issues. The second a teammate gives up you lose not only a person on the team but a vital part of Yuumi's kit is also lost. In Bot-Lane (where Yuumi is suppose to be supporting another player) it is even worse, where if you're carry isn't doing well, Yuumi has no tools to make it any better, and it's a lost lane.

Her kit is so powerful when used properly - even if she's a bit under-tuned at the moment, she has massive play potential. But with a toxic community this champ is almost completely destroyed, I've played 20 Yuumi Games as of the writing of this post, and 16 of them I had a marksman (carry I was supporting) either AFK or outright say I was useless and begin trolling. Sounds a bit like a self-fulfilling prophecy, don't you think? And that's not including the huge number of games people rage in champ select because I pick/declare Yuumi or the number of times my team intentionally banned her and so I just HAD to go Ezreal support (it worked, so don't judge!).

So hey, Yuumi is pretty cool, but maybe Riot should have done more to fix the toxicity of the game first... But then again, since the CEO's are dry humping their employees, I doubt that'll happen any time soon.

[Project] Akita

I know I mentioned I was working on a rhythm game, and after quite a lot of prototyping, it's official! Currently code named "[Project] Akita", Akita is a Rhythm / Visual Novel / RPG hybrid that combines the story elements expected of a multi-path VN, the progression and character building (as well as choice making) of an RPG, with the frantic and fun gameplay of a lane-based rhythm game! The current prototype is probably where I'll move into Alpha and start making an actually functioning game.

Sample Screen Shot showing the current visuals for [Project] Akita PROTO-5

[Project] Akita will have 3 different sections of play...
- Play point 1 will be the actual Rhythm Game itself. This will be initially pretty similar to other lane-based Rhythm games (think Guitar Hero, BeatMania, or more recently Musynx) but with a few things to mix these mechanics up with elements inspired by more free-form/specific input type games (Think Sound Vortex, Groove Coaster, Mai Mai).
- Play point 2 will be the RPG elements. Here you have a character and a full story to progress through, you act as a guardian spirit following other characters as you travel across maps, uncovering secrets and solving mysteries! Through doing so you'll improve various stats that help with progression, switch up the Rhythm Game aspect, and unlock new dialogue and areas.
- Play point 3 is the Visual Novel / Story aspect. When reaching points on different maps or triggering events characters will talk and interact with each other. During these segments the player will be able to make choices that will determine future characteristics of each character, such as how they treat you or how they react to other things you say.

I'll have a more complete post when I make a public release (I plan to release a very early alpha/prototype release soon[tm]). I've messed around with a lot of different mechanics, figuring out target platforms, writing story/lore, and of course actually building the prototypes. Currently our target platform is PC with android coming at a later date, keyboard or a multi-touch screen will be required to play.
